Mycobacterium tuberculosis

M. tuberculosis
The causative agent of tuberculosis (TB), one of the leading infectious disease killers worldwide. This obligate aerobic bacterium primarily affects the lungs but can cause disease in virtually any organ system.
WHO Priority: Critical

üìäPathogen Overview

Disease: Tuberculosis (TB)

Transmission: Airborne droplets

Host Range: Humans (primary), animals (M. bovis)

Clinical Significance: Leading cause of death from infectious disease globally

Drug Resistance: MDR-TB and XDR-TB strains pose significant clinical challenges

Pathogenesis

M. tuberculosis has evolved sophisticated mechanisms to survive within host macrophages. The bacterium's thick, waxy cell wall containing mycolic acids provides resistance to many antimicrobial agents and host immune responses.

Global Impact

TB remains a major global health threat, with an estimated 10.6 million new cases and 1.6 million deaths in 2021. The emergence of drug-resistant strains complicates treatment efforts worldwide.
